Entry 09 — Inequality

Chapter 8 — Populism in Malaysia

Populism has become one of the biggest political discussions around world in the 21st century. Whether it's Europe, the US or Asia, more political movements have started appealing directly to extreme groups while presenting certain groups as lesser, institutions or elites as the source of society's problems. The more connected the world becomes, the faster these ideas seem to spread across borders. Many see it as the issues of late stage globalisation.

Pieterse describes populism as something that cannot simply be placed on the political left or right. Instead, populism appears in different forms depending on the country, its history and the issues people care about the most. What remains consistent is the idea of creating an "us versus them" narrative that speaks directly to people's emotions rather than complicated political solutions.

Globalization Gives Populism a Bigger Audience

One thing I found interesting is that globalization doesn't only spread products, music or technology. It also spreads political ideas. In the age of information and misinformation. It is so easy to create echo chambers of what you want to hear instead of hearing non-bias opinions. A speech made halfway across the world can reach millions of people within minutes, making populism much easier to spread than ever before. The ideas of America can reach Asia, causing groups in Asia to think and feel the same way Americans feel. This same emotion can be felt around the world. Social media has easily made it accessable for everyone and anyone to form a bias opinion to anything.

"Worldwide, there is no reversal of globalisation." Pieterse, Globalization and Culture: Global Mélange, Populism Chapter 9

This is one the main reasons why populism is so apparent in the world today. It isn't a small news paper being thrown around, rather it appears as notifications for us to read before we even regulate oursevles in the morning.

Audience Attunement

As we learned previously, politics play a huge role in globalisation. Politicians don't give the same ideas all the time and the message always changes to appeal to a certain group of people. Thats why we see prominent figures more popular in countries, the opposite can happen for those that do not care for specific idealogies and beliefs.

I don't necessarily think this is unique to populism because almost every politician does it to some degree. However, social media has made audience attunement much easier. Everything that social media does today is the effect of curated searches and advertisment. The fact that it into our politics makes it harder for people to escape these echo chambers.

Key Idea Audience Attunement refers to changing the way a message is presented depending on who is listening. Rather than changing the goal, the communication itself is adapted to better connect with different groups.

Malaysia's Own Form of Populism

Malaysia has its smaller form of populism. Rather than focusing on immigration or globalisation alone, political discussions often revolve around race, religion and national identity. Ironically, even after many years of hybridity and cultural unity, race still comes up as a popular topic amongst extreme groups. The perspective of "purist" still form and because incredibly apparent with today's politics.

Recently, we have had much news coverage on immigration issues just like many other countires. This goes to show that globalisation through social media has created a divide between those that are inherently fine with immigration and others that prefer to not mix anymore.

Why I Think This Matters

This day and generation of populism is something that is seen in history. I believe it's a social construct that is very human and would happen regardless of globalisation. However, I think that globalisation and enhanced the speed at which it reaches everyone, inlcuding those with extreme beliefs and ideas. This makes globalisation somewhat dangerous in the wrong hands.

Personal thoughts

I think populism around the world will reach a breaking point at some point. Some countries may find a solution while others may break down in violence. I still think that globalization itself is one of the most important aspects of human history. It is important to know that globalisation will move forward and will always continue to share its technology, culture, tradtions and people around the world.
